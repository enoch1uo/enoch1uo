Details

In 2023, the community united as volunteers supported and uplifted thousands in need, finding their own sense of purpose along the way.
HandsOn volunteers gave their time and talent to serve in a variety of ways— empowering through education, uplifting elderly neighbours, sustainability initiatives and more.
Volunteer efforts were far and wide-ranging. From supporting refugee families and individuals experiencing unemployment, to activities protecting Hong Kong‘s nature and coastlines.
Children and teens across Hong Kong were supported through homework help, storytelling, languages, sports, games, and more. Young volunteers were also empowered to give back through service.
Volunteers were a lifeline for many Hong Kong charities, offering vital assistance as well as longer-term skills support across 3,620 service programs.
HandsOn developed social impact programs with more than 60 Hong Kong companies, empowering their volunteers through a range of skilled, impactful and meaningful initiatives.

Our signature programs

Our grant-funded community programs address existing gaps and respond to unmet needs. They go beyond the ordinary, as they are carefully designed to foster longer-term connections between volunteers and those we’re supporting.

With a focus on creating sustainable change, our initiatives aim to ignite a meaningful shift in behavior and attitudes that positively influences our city for years to come.

For the past three years, S&P Global has partnered with HandsOn Hong Kong as part of our commitment to help build inclusive, sustainable communities. Together, we have been able to contribute over 300 hours of volunteer services to have an impact on local farmland rehabilitation and coastal restoration efforts, as well as extending support and care for Hong Kong’s most vulnerable groups. Working together with shared goals and being able to lean on HandsOn’s expertise was key in empowering our colleagues to put ideas into action and create meaningful change.

The Hong Kong Society for the Deaf

Social Worker

Our weekly learning support group is run by a fantastic group of volunteers. HandsOn helps us to recruit passionate volunteers who are interested in serving children with hearing impairments—and who want to give back to the community. Volunteers get involved in homework and study support, languages, English tutoring, and more. Along the way, our services have got so much stronger with their support.
